On keeping a plain notebook

July 10, 2026

There is a certain comfort in a page that asks nothing of you. No colors, no badges, no notifications waiting in the margin. Just white space and the slow arrival of a thought.

I have kept the same kind of notebook for years now. Unruled, off-white, stitched rather than glued. The lack of lines means the writing can wander, and it usually does.

Reading without a highlighter

July 3, 2026

For a long stretch I marked up every book I read, convinced the ink was proof of attention. Lately I have put the highlighter down and found that the sentences I actually remember are the ones I let pass by quietly.

Minimalism in reading is less about owning fewer books and more about letting each page breathe before moving to the next.
